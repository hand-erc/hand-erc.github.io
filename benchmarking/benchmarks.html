Multi-Level Hand Benchmarks

Benchmarks for evaluating robot hands at the application, system, hand, and component level.
HAND ERC — System Integration Team

This page presents a hierarchical set of benchmarks for evaluating dexterous robotic hands. The benchmarks listed here are test protocols that yield performance scores using one or more metrics. Some metrics can be evaluated without performing a test. Due to the complexity of a fully integrated dexterous hand system, we have broken down hand benchmarking into four levels: Application, System, Hand, and Component.

Hand and Component-Level benchmarks are more directly affected by design decisions. The complexity of a robot hand creates an attribution problem, where system designers have difficulty understanding the sensitivity of system-level performance to their decisions. These benchmarks will help us make those connections.
